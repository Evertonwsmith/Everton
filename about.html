About myself


I grew up in West Kelowna, BC, where I started playing hockey at the age of five. A few years later my grandfather would start teaching me to golf. When I was 13 I started to teach myself how to play the guitar, and join the school band program. In high school, I continued with the music program. Through this I became a member of a few different small ensembles that would have the opportunity to play at local wineries and cafes. We also found a lot of success in festivals and competitions, including winning the B.C. Interior Jazz Festival twice, and winning a Gold Standard Award at Musicfest Nationals in 2013.

By the end of high school, I had obtained a 4.0 GPA, Dean's List and Honour Roll. In 2014 I started my post-secondary education at the University of British Columbia Okanagan, Kelowna BC, where I obtained a B.Sc. in Computer Science. After graduating, I worked as a Software Developer for nearly 6 years. Now, I have decided to further my education by pursuing a diploma in Electrical Engineering Technology at SAIT, and am currenly in my first semester.

Outside of school, I stay busy with a wide range of passions. I’m a lifelong musician and crafter with a love for the outdoors, specifically camping, hiking, and golf. I also have a deep appreciation for the strategy found in board games, card games and video games.
